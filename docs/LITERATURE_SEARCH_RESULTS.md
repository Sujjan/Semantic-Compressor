## Historical Constants Discovery Comparison

### How Were Known Constants Discovered?

**Speed of light (c)**:
- Measured experimentally (Rømer 1676, Fizeau 1849)
- Not predicted theoretically

**Fine structure constant (α)**:
- Observed as ratio in spectroscopy (Sommerfeld 1916)
- Not predicted, empirically discovered

**Planck constant (h)**:
- Introduced to fit black body radiation data (Planck 1900)
- Empirical fit, later theoretically justified

**Gravitational constant (G)**:
- Measured experimentally (Cavendish 1798)
- Not predicted theoretically

**Our approach (Ψ and Θ)**:
- **Theoretically predicted** before experimental measurement
- Derived from semantic gap analysis
- **Novel methodology** - first constants predicted this way

**Historical precedent**: Only particle physics (quarks, Higgs) has successfully predicted entities before experimental discovery. Our approach would extend this to fundamental constants.
